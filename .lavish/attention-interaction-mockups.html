Pick the expand interaction

Four real patterns for what happens when you click a step in the attention pipeline (the QKᵗ score matrix step is the clickable one in every mockup below, styled the way it would actually look on the site). Click a highlighted node to try it, then use Choose this pattern on the one that feels right.

tip: you can also select and annotate any element directly to leave a note.

1. Master-detail panel belowrecommended

The pipeline stays fixed as a persistent rail. Clicking a step drops a connector line into a full-width panel beneath with room for staged matrix animations.

Trade-off: excellent context + most room for number choreography, but two zones on screen instead of one.

2. Accordion-in-placezero context loss

The clicked node itself grows into a wide inline card; siblings compress to icon-only chips. Nothing ever leaves the row.

Trade-off: nothing leaves view, but the detail area fights the row for width — cramped proportions for a matrix-heavy scene.

3. Semantic zoomshowpiece

Clicking flies the camera into the node — the tiny glyph morphs into the full scene. Pipeline shrinks to a breadcrumb pinned above.

Trade-off: the most dramatic "the icon IS the data" moment, but the highest build cost and hardest to keep smooth.

4. Overlay modalmobile fallback

Node click opens a centered dialog card, background dims. Cheapest and roomiest, but severs spatial context from the diagram.

Trade-off: most screen space of all four, but reads as "a dialog popped up," not "a diagram unfolded."
